Game setup

How to set up a new Football Manager save has been covered extensively in the past so for the scouting map I have only looked at youth production, not league results or transfer activity.

Summary

Larger databases result in more youth production, but the difference is smaller for high potential players, and you get diminishing returns for very large databases. Adding a wide range of playable leagues or adding players across the whole world increases the variety of nations producing good prospects, but spreads out production of good prospects rather than simply adding more.

Nations that aren’t worth scouting on average can still produce good prospects occasionally, and this seems more likely on larger databases.

This may also make scouting harder as there will be more nations to cover and far more low potential players as well.

Suggested setups:

If you want the absolute highest youth production– load the largest database you can, with playable leagues, but this can add 2 or 3 hours to a season even on a high end PC.

If you want varied youth production without the performance hit – load a few playable leagues (e.g. top 5 in Europe), but add a large database of additional players.

If you want the best performance – Only load leagues you want to play in, but you can add some additional players without losing much performance. Adding international & high reputation players will increase your database size and youth production. If you only add specific countries, focus on scouting those.

Database size

Larger database adds more players, but it’s a small difference compared to having the league playable or not. I used large for most of my tests as it didn’t change performance much on my PC.

League type

Not loaded – low number of newgens.
View-only – same newgen production as not loaded. Some performance cost.
Playable – much higher newgen production, high performance cost.

Advanced setup

This allows you to add players to the database without adding the leagues they play in. This can greatly increase newgen production & expand the range of nations producing good prospects without the performance cost of playable leagues. The options range from adding top players only, to adding everyone playing in the nation.

Playable leagues vs loading players only

When you load a league as playable, FM will generate some newgens right at the start of the game. The numbers and nationalities of these players vary by which leagues you load. I haven’t been able to work out why or how the game decides where to create these players, but they make a difference to later newgen production. It can add players to nations other than the selected playable league in some circumstances.

Examples

Numbers for example nations on different setups:

Brazil – Brazil will always produce a good number of prospects regardless of database size. On the smallest database, non-playable, they averaged 22 players per intake with 11 having high potential. On the largest, 7,700 players per intake with 35 having good potential.

Nigeria – A nation with high youth rating, but no option for playable leagues. On the smallest database, they weren’t worth scouting with an average of 1.8 players per intake and only 1 good prospect across 10 simulations. In the largest, they’re one of the better nations with 527 per intake and 2.8 with good potential.

Japan – Another nation with high youth rating but no playable leagues. However they show the effect of other playable leagues described above. On the largest database (without playable leagues), they produced 67 per intake with 2 having good potential. On a mid-sized database, but with all leagues playable, they produced more at 106 per intake with 3.6 being high potential.

Algeria – A nation that seems only worth scouting when loading a very large database. In most setups their youth production was very low, however when loading all available players, they fell just outside the top 20 with 147 players per intake and 1.8 with good potential.

Some setups I tested:

Large database, 1 playable league, top division & international players loaded – 104,000 starting player count, 17,000 newgens per year, 249 with 150+ potential and 13 with 180+. 63 different nations producing at least 1 150+ PA player.

Large database, 1 playable league, all additional players loaded – 458,000 starting player count, 101,000 newgens per year, 289 with 150+ potential and 18 with 180+. 72 different nations producing at least 1 150+ PA player.

Large database, all leagues playable, top division & international players loaded – 202,000 starting player count, 45,000 newgens per year, 283 with 150+ potential and 14 with 180+. 70 different nations producing at least 1 150+ PA player.
